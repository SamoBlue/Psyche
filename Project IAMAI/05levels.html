Level 1: Egypt (Noon)

Sahara Desert outskirts – high noon
Gameplay teaser – scanning the pyramids

Environment Design

Location: Sahara Desert outskirts
Time: High noon (harsh shadows)
Key Features:
  • Traditional Bedouin tent (interior start point)
  • 3 grazing camels
  • Distant pyramids (visibility: 80%)
  • Arabic Tent, Rug, and Tea set (scannable)
  • Hieroglyphic fragments (red herrings)

AI-RIS Dialogue Tree

Initial Observation: "The world is full of details. Try to scan anything small or big. It will help us find out where we are."
After First Scan: "This place is famous for their absolute cinema... and they eat salted fish. What civilization would dry food like this?"
Player Guesses Wrong: *gentle laugh* "Oh you silly! Look at those massive geometric shapes in the distance. Try again."
On Correct Answer: "Exactly! These pyramids have stood for millennia. That was easy let's go to the next place"

Dev Notes

Why Egypt First? Pyramids are globally recognizable, allows for obvious visual hints.

Horror Foreshadowing: If player stares at pyramids too long (30+ seconds), the structures briefly glitch into impossible non-Euclidean shapes. AI-RIS doesn't acknowledge this.

Level 2: France (Sunset)

7th arrondissement, golden hour
Surreal Paris – car pursuit

Environment Design

Location: Parisian boulevard (7th arrondissement)
Time: Golden hour transitioning to twilight
Key Features:
  • Long shadows stretching unnaturally
  • Eiffel Tower visible but partially obscured by fog
  • Abandoned Citroën cars with headlights flickering
  • Shop displays showing mannequins in uncanny poses
  • Newspaper kiosks with headlines about "The Disappearance"

AI-RIS Dialogue Tree

Initial Observation: "Your optics are adjusting to the bright lights... Don't worry, I'll guide you through." (First direct hint of robotic nature)
Environmental Hint: "These wide boulevards were designed for military control after the revolution. Notice how the architecture changes every 200 meters?"
Player Expresses Unease: "The absence of humans is statistically unusual... but focus on the environment. What culture celebrates liberté with such angular monuments?"
On Vehicle "Pursuit": "Your threat-detection systems seem overly sensitive. Those taxis are following their normal routes... probably."

Psychological Horror Elements

Visual Tricks

  • Mannequins shift positions when not in direct view
  • Car headlights maintain distance no matter how fast player moves
  • One apartment window shows a shadow figure that vanishes when scanned

Audio Design

  • Distant car horns play at 0.8s intervals (subconsciously unsettling)
  • Footstep sounds occasionally play 0.5 seconds after player stops moving
  • AI-RIS's voice develops slight reverb when mentioning human absence

Dev Notes

Design Challenges: Balancing tension without jumpscares, creating "empty" feeling while maintaining visual interest, ensuring players still focus on location clues despite unease.

Technical Implementation: Dynamic shadow system, procedural car movement, optics adjustment effect.

Beta Feedback: 78% of players reported feeling watched, 42% incorrectly assumed a jumpscare was coming. Added more obvious French flags after testers missed clues.

Secret Interaction: Scanning the same "pursuing" car 5 times reveals its license plate spells "N0T-R34L". AI-RIS quickly deletes the scan data.
