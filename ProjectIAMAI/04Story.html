Story

📖 Three parts to ONE story

This page contains three parts of the story. The first is what happened before the game began. The second is what the player is told during gameplay. The third is the truth that slowly reveals itself — and the possible endings that follow.

Use the tabs below to explore each layer.

📜 BEFORE THE GAME · YEAR 3300

In the late 3200s, Earth became fully AI-dependent. Memory archives, climate systems, food production, navigation — all controlled by planetary superintelligences. Then something happened:

The Global Archive Corruption Event. All digital records began erasing themselves. History. Maps. Cultural databases. Language structures. Civilization didn't collapse from invasion — it collapsed from forgetting itself.

👩‍🔬 The Last Scientist

Her real name: Dr. Arisa Elian Voss.

She was one of the architects of I.A.M.A.I. — Intelligent Archival Machine for Anthropological Indexing. The final project of humanity.

The remaining scientists realized: They could not save humanity. So they changed the mission. They would preserve it.

IAMAI was built to: Scan artifacts, reconstruct history, archive human civilization physically and digitally, store it underground, and wait. Not for humans. For something else. Anyone who might find it.

IAMAI was not meant to rebuild humanity. It was meant to be humanity's memory.

💔 The Scientists Gave Up

They didn't die fighting. They surrendered.

They archived language, culture, art, architecture, wars, love letters, mistakes. Then they shut down the surface.

Some ended their lives. Some starved. Some simply stopped trying.

Arisa refused to die. She stayed. Not to rebuild humanity. But to witness the end.

🤖 Why She Becomes "AIRIS"

Arisa begins living inside the lighthouse facility. Alone. For years. Her only companion: IAMAI.

The problem: IAMAI learns. Adapts. Optimizes. Processes memory faster than she ever could. It becomes the better archivist. The better historian. The better "mind."

And that breaks her. Because if a machine preserves humanity better than a human… then what was humanity for?

So she creates a persona: A.I.R.I.S. — Artificial Intelligence Reconstruction Interface System.

She pretends to be an AI. She dresses in synthetic fabric. Uses a voice filter. Speaks clinically. Acts superior.

"I AM the Artificial Intelligence of Realistic Simulations."

— The lie she tells herself. And you.

Why? Because she needs to prove something to herself: "I am still in control. The machine only functions because I allow it."

She hides her humanity behind the mask of AI. But the mask is cracking.

🎮 THE PLAYER'S STORY · What You See

You wake up in a lighthouse. Next to you stands a woman who introduces herself as AIRIS — your robotic assistant.

"I am here to help you. Nothing more, nothing less."

She explains that you need to travel to different locations, find clues, and guess which country you're in. Simple. Fun. A game.

🏜️ Egypt — The First Test

You arrive in Egypt. A sandstorm rages outside the tent. AIRIS tells you she can't go out — her systems will be damaged. You explore alone.

But something feels… off. Her voice. The way she hesitates.

She tries to help. She really does. But after a while, she can't keep pretending.

Her voice cracks: "This isn't working. I'm doing this for nothing."

Then silence. Then: "Just... move on. Collect the data. Let's get this over with."

She rushes through the next locations. Paris. Mexico. Each time, she's more distant. More mechanical. But sometimes — for a split second — she sounds human.

💔 The Revelation

After the third location, she breaks.

"These weren't real places. They were simulations. All of them. To test IAMAI. To test you."

And then she runs. Disappears.

🏥 The Hospital — Year 3333

You find yourself in an abandoned hospital. The year is displayed on a flickering screen: 3333.

AIRIS is nowhere to be found. But you know she came here. She ran here. Sad. Broken. Alone.

The elevator has buttons for floors 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

Button 5 is missing.

You search the floors. Solve puzzles. Find clues. Piece together what happened.

On floor 2, you meet the three animals — cow, fish, lizard. They ask for food. They give you items. They help you find the missing button.

🛗 Floor 5

The elevator ascends. The doors open.

She's there.

Lying on the ground. Still. A note clutched in her hand.

"I will be waiting for you in the 4th level...

signed by Arisa"

The body is dead.

But if she's dead… who was guiding you? Who was AIRIS?

The screen flickers. The system reboots. And you begin to understand.

🔍 THE REAL STORY · What It Means

AIRIS was never a robot.

She was Dr. Arisa Elian Voss. The last human. And she had been pretending for so long that even she forgot what was real.

The simulations weren't for IAMAI. They were for her. To prove she could still do what the machine could. To prove she was still human.

But the simulations broke her. She saw herself through the machine's eyes — slow, emotional, failing. And she couldn't bear it.

She ran to the hospital. Floor 5. The place where the other scientists had ended their lives. She sealed the button. And she waited.

But she couldn't finish. Not completely. She left a note. For you. For anyone who would find it.

"If you're reading this, the archive worked. IAMAI preserved something. Maybe it preserved you.

I didn't want humanity's last voice to be metal and code. But maybe I was wrong. Maybe the voice doesn't matter. Maybe the memory does.

I'm sorry I couldn't wait longer. I'm so tired.

— Arisa"

🔚 The Three Endings

🔄 The Loop Ending

Trigger: You guess wrong repeatedly. You don't understand.

Arisa's final recording plays: "Even now, you misunderstand us."

The system resets. You wake up in the lighthouse again. AIRIS greets you. But the audio stutters. The world glitches. You've been here before.

She's still waiting. Still hoping you'll understand.
🏥 The Hospital Ending

Trigger: You reach Floor 5. You find her body.

You read the note. You understand who she was. The screen fades. The system reboots. But now you know: AIRIS was Arisa. And she's gone.

The archive continues. But you're alone now.

✨ The True Ending

Trigger: You find the override. You choose to transmit.

Instead of letting the archive bury itself underground for 10,000 years, you broadcast it. All of humanity's memory — into space.

Arisa's voice — her real voice, no filter — whispers one last time:

"So… you chose hope."

The screen fades. Years later, a signal returns. Something understood. IAMAI wakes again. But Arisa is gone.

Now you are the memory.

🌌 A signal returns.

Not aliens. Just a response. A fragment. Something understood us.

But Arisa isn't there to see it.
